Your Guide to Greece

From the Acropolis to the Athens Riviera, here’s everything you need to make the most of your trip — where to stay, where to eat, where to party, what to see, and how to sort your visa. Most of these spots are minutes from The Margi Farm or a short ride into the city.

Getting Here & Around

Getting There

Fly into Athens International Airport “Eleftherios Venizelos” (ATH) — Greece's main hub, with direct flights from across Europe, the Middle East, and North America.

The Margi Farm is just ~15 minutes from the airport.

Getting Around

Uber works throughout Athens (it dispatches licensed local taxis), and pre-booked private transfers are easy to arrange in advance.

Renting a car is simple too — the farm has parking, and it's a short drive to the Athens Riviera (~25–30 min to central Athens).

Money

The currency is the Euro (€). Cards are widely accepted; it’s handy to carry some cash for tavernas, taxis, and small shops.

What to See

Ancient Athens

The Acropolis & Parthenon, the Acropolis Museum, and the Ancient Agora — the heart of the classical city. Go early to beat the heat and the crowds.

Old Town & Markets

Plaka and Monastiraki — winding lanes, tavernas, rooftop views of the Acropolis, and the famous flea market.

Athens Riviera

Vouliagmeni, Glyfada, and the thermal Lake Vouliagmeni — beaches and seaside swims just minutes from the venue.

Cape Sounion

The clifftop Temple of Poseidon at sunset, about 45 minutes south along the coast — one of Greece’s great views.

Islands & Day Trips

Hop to the Saronic islands (Aegina, Hydra, Poros) by ferry, or venture to Delphi and Nafplio for a day of history.

How to Get a Visa

Greece is in the EU’s Schengen Area, so what you need depends on your nationality. Here’s the short version — always confirm with the official sources for your passport.

No Visa Needed

Citizens of the EU/EEA & Switzerland, and visa-exempt countries like the US, UK, Canada, and Australia, need no visa for short visits.

Non-EU visitors may stay up to 90 days within any 180-day period across the Schengen Area.

ETIAS

ETIAS is a quick online travel authorisation (not a visa) for visa-exempt travellers. It is expected to launch in late 2026, with a transitional grace period.

For an early-September 2026 trip it is not expected to be required yet — but check the official EU site closer to your trip: travel-europe.europa.eu/etias.

Schengen Visa (Type C)

If your nationality isn’t visa-exempt, apply for a Schengen short-stay visa at the Greek embassy/consulate or visa centre where you live.

Useful documents: our wedding invitation, hotel booking, return flights, proof of funds, and travel insurance. Apply 6–8 weeks ahead. Greek MFA →

Passport Validity

For all non-EU travellers, your passport must be valid for at least 3 months beyond your departure from the Schengen Area, and issued within the last 10 years.

Check yours now — and renew early if it’s close to either limit.

This is a friendly starting point, not official advice — please verify the requirements for your specific nationality via official EU and Greek government sources before booking travel.
