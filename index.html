Travel Details

✈ Getting There

Fly into Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY) — Beirut's main airport with direct flights from major cities worldwide.

Airlines with direct routes include MEA, Turkish Airlines, Emirates, Air France, and Lufthansa.

🏨 Where to Stay

We recommend staying in Batroun (near the venue) or Beirut (about 50 minutes south). Batroun is a charming coastal town with great boutique hotels.

Hotel block details coming soon!

🚗 Getting Around

Uber and Bolt are widely available in Beirut. You can also arrange a private driver for day trips.

The city is best explored on foot in the central neighborhoods.

📄 Visa & Currency

Many nationalities receive a visa on arrival. The local currency is the Lebanese Pound (LBP), but USD is widely accepted.

We recommend bringing US dollars in cash.

Things to Do in Lebanon

Make the most of your visit

🏛
Sightseeing

Byblos (Jbeil)

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Explore the Crusader castle, ancient port, and charming souks.

🏔
Nature

Jeita Grotto

A breathtaking limestone cave system just 20 minutes from Beirut. A must-see natural wonder.

🍴
Food & Drink

Beirut Food Scene

From rooftop restaurants in Gemmayzeh to traditional mezze in Achrafieh — Beirut's food scene is world-class.

🏙
Day Trip

Baalbek Temples

The largest Roman temples ever built. An awe-inspiring UNESCO World Heritage site in the Bekaa Valley.

🌴
Nature

Cedars of God

Visit Lebanon's iconic ancient cedar trees — a UNESCO site and the symbol of the nation.

🌊
Nightlife

Mar Mikhael & Gemmayzeh

Beirut's vibrant nightlife district. Rooftop bars, live music, and an electric atmosphere every night.
